The Psychology of Age Gaps

Research on age-gap relationships reveals interesting patterns:

Relationship Satisfaction

Studies published in the American Psychological Association journals show couples with gaps under 10 years report similar satisfaction levels to same-age couples.

chart

Divorce Rates

Research indicates divorce risk increases by 3% per year of age difference. A 5-year gap shows 18% higher risk, while 10-year gaps show 39% higher risk compared to same-age couples.

globe

Cultural Factors

In collectivist cultures (Asia, Middle East), larger age gaps are more accepted. In individualist Western cultures, same-age partnerships are more common.

time

Historical Trends

Average age gaps have decreased over the past century. In 1900, 5-10 year gaps were common; today 2-3 years is typical in developed nations.
