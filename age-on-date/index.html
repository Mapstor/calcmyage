When to Use an Age on Date Calculator

passport

Passport Applications

The U.S. State Department and passport agencies worldwide require your exact age to determine fees, validity periods, and application requirements. Child passports (under 16) have different rules than adult passports.

ring

Wedding Planning

Legal marriage age requirements vary by jurisdiction. In most U.S. states, 18 is the marriage age without parental consent (NCSL). Knowing exact ages on your wedding date is essential for legal documentation.

history

Historical Events

Ever wondered how old you were during major events? Find out your age during the moon landing (1969), the fall of the Berlin Wall (1989), or the COVID-19 pandemic declaration (March 11, 2020).

school

School Enrollment

Schools use age cutoff dates to determine grade placement. According to the National Center for Education Statistics, cutoff dates range from July 1 to January 1, depending on the state.

Age Verification for Legal Purposes

Many legal documents and applications require your exact age on a specific date. Here's when precision matters:

legal

Court Proceedings

Age at the time of an incident can determine jurisdiction (juvenile vs. adult court), sentencing guidelines, and statute of limitations considerations.

work

Employment Eligibility

Child labor laws restrict work hours and job types based on age. The U.S. Department of Labor sets federal minimums that states can exceed.

money

Social Security Benefits

Your full retirement age depends on your birth year. Benefits can start at 62 (reduced) or be delayed until 70 (increased).

health

Medicare Enrollment

Medicare eligibility begins at age 65. Your initial enrollment period starts 3 months before your 65th birthday month.
